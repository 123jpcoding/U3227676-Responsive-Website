Judo

Judo, meaning ‘gentle way’, is a modern Japanese martial art and Olympic combat sport (Budokan Judo Club Australia, 2022). The sport is competitive, with an objective to “cleanly throw, to pin, or to master one’s opponent, the latter being done by applying pressure to arm joints or to the neck to cause the opponent to yield” (Burdick, 2018).

Marist Judo Club operates on a basis that Judo is for everyone, meaning the club caters to children, teenagers and adults alike at all ability levels. Marist Judo has fully qualified coaches and teach on Monday and Wednesday evenings. There are two separate training sessions, with a Junior class focused on children aged 8-12, and a Senior lesson training teens, young adults and adults. Marist Judo Club is an affiliated member of Judo ACT and the Australian Judo Federation, meaning students can participate in competitions held throughout the year by those bodies.
