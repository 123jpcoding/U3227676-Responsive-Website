Our History

Judo, meaning ‘gentle way’, is a modern Japanese martial art and Olympic combat sport (Budokan Judo Club Australia, 2022). The sport is competitive, with an objective to “cleanly throw, to pin, or to master one’s opponent, the latter being done by applying pressure to arm joints or to the neck to cause the opponent to yield” (Burdick, 2018). Marist Judo Club operates on a basis that Judo is for everyone, meaning the club caters to children, teenagers and adults alike at all ability levels. Marist Judo has fully qualified coaches and teach on Monday and Wednesday evenings. There are two separate training sessions, with a Junior class focused on children aged 8-12, and a Senior lesson training teens, young adults and adults. Marist Judo Club is an affiliated member of Judo ACT and the Australian Judo Federation, meaning students can participate in competitions held throughout the year by those bodies.

Meet Our Coaches

Coach Victoria

21-year old Victoria has been learning Judo since she was 5 years old at Marist Judo Club. She has competed multiple times at local and national competitions, as well as the 2017 Oceania tournament in Tonga. Her Judo skills have transferred to other sports, and she loves the opportunity to teach these skills to the next generation.

Coach Patrick

Patrick has been coaching for 9 years and has represented Marist at a number of competitions. He has competed at a national and international level, representing Australia in global Judo tournaments. He has recently been applying his skills in MMA fighting, coming third at the most recent world championships in Amstterdam.

Coach Steven

Steven Hill has been practicing and competiting in Judo for 45 years. He has been an Australian Champion multiple times, and has repeatedly competed for Australia internationally. He was selected for the 2002 Edinburgh Commonwealth Games, as well as the Australian Olympic team. He has ample experience coaching a variety of skill levels, coaching student at Marist Judo club, as well as squads selected for the ACT and Australia. He also has experience coaching students in MMA.

Coach Matthew

Matthew Hill has been practicing Judo for 40 years and coaching for 30. He has represented the sport at local, state, national and international levels, and has coached students up to a national level.

Coach Thomas

Thomas Hill has been performing Judo for over 40 years. He has had a highly successful Judo career, competing at the 2000 Olympics, and winning a gold medal at the 2002 Commonwealth Games. He has since prioritised coaching, acting as the Head Coach of the Australian Olympic team. He also has experience coaching MMA.
